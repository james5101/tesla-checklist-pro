# What's actually included with a new Tesla in 2026 — research brief

Internal research brief for an SEO-led explainer page on TeslaChecklistPro. Funnels readers to `/accessories` (the items below that are NOT included are the items that page recommends). The published article is voiced for a brand-new owner who has paid the deposit and is now reading the order summary on the Tesla app for the first time.

Brief written: 2026-05-01. Contents current as of: 2026-04-30. Tesla changes inclusions quietly — re-verify the per-model table at every quarterly refresh.

---

## Methodology

- Primary sources: Tesla support pages (tesla.com/support/*), Tesla shop product pages, teslamotorsclub.com, cybertruckownersclub.com, teslaownersonline.com, r/TeslaLounge / r/cybertruck.
- Secondary sources used for date-stamping changes (not as standalone evidence): InsideEVs, Electrek, TechCrunch, Teslarati, autoevolution, Drive Tesla.
- Forum-thread date range used for current-inclusion claims: 2024-01 through 2026-04. Older threads cited only for historical-change context (the 2022 Mobile Connector removal).
- An item appears in the "included" column only when (a) Tesla support / shop says so explicitly, OR (b) two independent owner threads from the relevant model + year confirm it shipped in the car.

## The short answer (1 sentence per model)

- **Model S (2024–2026):** car keys (2 cards), J1772 adapter, USB drive in the glovebox, owner documentation. No Mobile Connector, no Wall Connector, no NEMA 14-50 adapter, no all-weather mats unless purchased.
- **Model 3 Highland (2024+):** same baseline as Model S. No Mobile Connector unless added at order. J1772 adapter has been intermittently reported missing on some Highland deliveries — owners flag this as a "check the glovebox before signing" item.
- **Model X (2024–2026):** same baseline as Model S. Tri-zone climate, panoramic roof and 22-speaker audio are factory-equipment, not "accessories" in the box.
- **Model Y Juniper (2025+):** same baseline. Confirmed J1772 adapter included. Some Launch Series Junipers shipped with a free Air Compressor + Tire Repair Kit 3.0; standard Junipers did not.
- **Cybertruck:** baseline (post-Foundation, late-2024 onward) ships with carpet floor mats, mobile connector, charging adapter, USB drive, and 2 key cards. Foundation Series adds: all-weather (rubber) mats, gear-locker dividers, glass-roof sunshade, D-rings, L-track hooks, L-track bottle opener, centre-console tray, off-road light bar, Powershare hardware, Universal Wall Connector, lifetime Premium Connectivity, FSD.

## Historical changes worth knowing

### April 2022 — Mobile Connector removed as standard
Tesla removed the Gen 2 Mobile Connector bundle from new orders effective **April 17, 2022**. Orders placed before that date still received it; orders placed after did not. Initial price after removal was $400; after public backlash Musk dropped it to $200 (now ~$230–$300 on the Tesla shop, current 2026). Sources:
- TechCrunch, "Tesla pulls the plug on the Gen 2 Mobile Connector Bundle" (April 18, 2022)
- InsideEVs, "Tesla Orders Starting April 17 Won't Include Mobile Connector" (April 2022)
- TMC announcement thread, "Mobile Connector no longer included with new cars [posted 04/16/2022]" (April 16, 2022)
- Teslarati (April 2022)

### 2019 — NEMA 14-50 adapter dropped from Mobile Connector bundle
Predates the Mobile Connector removal itself. From 2019 the Mobile Connector bundle ships with a NEMA 5-15 (standard household) adapter only; the NEMA 14-50 (dryer outlet) adapter is a separate $35 purchase. Source: TMC, "NEMA 14-50 adapter no longer included with vehicles" (2019); kbelectricpa.com summary.

### 2023+ — Mat configurations across refreshes
Highland (Model 3, late 2023) and Juniper (Model Y, early 2025) shipped with new factory carpet mats sized for the redesigned floor pans. Pre-Highland / pre-Juniper aftermarket all-weather mats do not fit. Source: shop.tesla.com Highland and Juniper-specific mat SKUs; multiple TMC fitment threads (2024–2025).

### Late 2024 — Cybertruck Foundation Series ended; standard trim launched
Tesla shifted from Foundation-only deliveries to a standard trim around October 2024. The standard trim removed all-weather mats, light bar, FSD, Powershare hardware, Universal Wall Connector, and reduced bed tie-down rings from 8 to 4. Sources: autoevolution (Oct 2024), notebookcheck (2024), Drive Tesla (2024), Electrek "Tesla is buffing Foundation Series badges off Cybertrucks to sell them as regular trucks" (Dec 14, 2024).

### 2024 — Launch promo: Tire Repair Kit included with some deliveries
Tesla included an Air Compressor + Tire Repair Kit 3.0 with some 2024 Model 3 Highland deliveries and some 2025 Model Y Juniper Launch Series deliveries as a launch promo. **Not standard.** Sources: Adhesive and Sealant Council write-up (2024); Tesla Owners Online, "Portable Air Compressor 'comes with Tire Repair Kit' in '26 Launch Y?" (2025).

### NACS transition (2024+) — J1772 confusion
With most non-Tesla brands moving to NACS, the J1772 adapter is becoming less essential but is still shipping in new Teslas (per shop.tesla.com product page wording "included with every Tesla vehicle delivery"). Some Highland delivery threads in 2024 report the adapter was missing — verify before signing. Source: TMC Highland Waiting Room threads (2024).

## How to verify what your car will come with BEFORE delivery

1. **Tesla app — Order page → "Final Documents."** The Order Agreement lists trim, options, and any added accessories as line items (e.g. "Mobile Connector $230" appears separately if added).
2. **Pre-delivery checklist in the Tesla app.** Tesla rolls out a per-region pre-delivery task list; this confirms what you owe and gives a final pickup-day window. Source: tesla.com/support/interactive-videos/get-to-know-your-pre-delivery-tasks.
3. **Delivery advisor message thread.** Owners on TMC and Tesla Owners Online repeatedly recommend asking the advisor in writing: *"Please confirm whether the J1772 adapter, USB drive, and floor mats are in the car at handover."* The written reply is leverage if anything is missing on day one.
4. **Window sticker (Monroney Label).** Issued at delivery, not before — but it is the legal record of factory equipment. Source: tesla.com/support/after-taking-delivery.

Threads where pre-confirmation saved owners from a missing item:
- TMC, "Picking up 2026 MY soon...Delivery Checklist?" (2026) — owner reports asking delivery advisor about J1772 in advance and finding it absent in two prior pickups.
- TMC, Highland Waiting Room, page 50 (2024) — multiple posts about J1772 adapter missing on initial Highland deliveries; owners who pre-confirmed got a replacement adapter shipped within a week.

## Common confusions to debunk

### "Mobile Connector" vs "Wall Connector" vs "Universal Wall Connector" vs "CHAdeMO adapter"

- **Mobile Connector**: portable, plugs into a household or 240 V outlet. ~$230. Adds up to 30 mph of range on a 240 V outlet. NOT included with new cars (since April 2022). Source: tesla.com/support/charging/mobile-connector.
- **Wall Connector**: hardwired Level 2 charger you mount in a garage. Adds up to 44 mph. NACS-only handle. Hardware ~$475 + electrician install. NEVER included.
- **Universal Wall Connector**: same as Wall Connector but with a built-in J1772 handle for non-Tesla EVs. Same charging speed. Included with **Cybertruck Foundation Series only**, separately shipped — never with S/3/X/Y.
- **CHAdeMO adapter**: legacy Japanese DC fast-charging adapter. Niche, third-party. Tesla discontinued its own CHAdeMO adapter years ago. Not relevant to most 2026 buyers.
- **J1772 adapter**: small AC adapter that lets the car plug into non-Tesla Level 2 public chargers. Included with new cars per shop.tesla.com product copy; intermittently reported missing on 2024 Highland deliveries — verify.

### 110 V vs 240 V — "what charger Tesla ships now"
Since April 2022, Tesla ships **nothing** with new S/3/X/Y. Cybertruck ships a Powershare Mobile Connector. The Mobile Connector when purchased separately includes a NEMA 5-15 (110 V household) adapter only — to charge at 240 V you also need to buy a NEMA 14-50 adapter (~$35) or install a Wall Connector.

### NEMA 14-50 adapter
NOT included with the Mobile Connector. Sold separately on shop.tesla.com (~$35). Was bundled pre-2019. Common misunderstanding because YouTube delivery videos from 2018–2021 still show it in the bundle. Source: TMC "NEMA 14-50 adapter no longer included with vehicles" (2019).

### Spare tire
No Tesla has ever shipped a spare tire. Model 3, Y, and Cybertruck use foam-lined tires that are incompatible with green Slime sealant — owners are explicitly warned against using sealant-based "tire repair" products that ICE drivers might be familiar with. Source: TMC, "Tesla Tire Repair Kit same as Slime Tire Repair Kit?" (2024).

## Gaps and caveats

1. **No single Tesla support page enumerates "what's in the car at delivery."** Tesla's `/support/taking-delivery` and `/support/delivery-day` pages walk through the *process* but never list contents. Every per-model bundle line above is reconstructed from the Cybertruck FAQ (the only model with an explicit list), the Tesla shop product copy (J1772 adapter), and owner-thread confirmation. If the published article needs Tesla to say something authoritatively, only the Cybertruck list is fully covered.

2. **USB drive specs are forum-confirmed, not Tesla-confirmed.** Tesla does not publish that the factory drive is a 128 GB Samsung Bar Plus. CTOC threads name the drive; Tesla support pages on Sentry/Dashcam refer only to "a USB drive in the glovebox." Treat the brand/capacity claim as MED confidence.

3. **Standard Cybertruck inclusion list is recent.** Tesla shifted from Foundation-only to standard Cybertruck around October 2024. Forum coverage of standard-trim deliveries is thinner than Foundation coverage. If the article publishes after another quarterly refresh, re-verify the standard-trim row before pushing.

4. **Highland J1772 adapter status is genuinely conflicting.** The Tesla shop page says "included with every Tesla vehicle delivery." Multiple 2024 Highland threads report it absent. We can't reconcile these from the outside. The article should phrase it: "Per Tesla's product copy, included. Per several 2024 Highland delivery reports, sometimes missing — verify on-site."
