# Model Y delivery-defect research — 2026-04-19

## Summary

Broad sweep of TeslaMotorsClub (TMC) and InsideEVs for Model Y delivery-day defects, with emphasis on the 2025+ Juniper refresh. Recurring themes: misalignment around the new front and rear light bars and the ambient dash strip, liftgate and trunk bump-stop fit, TCU/WiFi dropouts on Juniper, phone-key unpair issues in home WiFi range, factory-disabled rear defrost, and headlights arriving aimed too high. Reddit (r/TeslaLounge) was not accessible to the search agent on this run — findings below lean on TMC primary threads and InsideEVs editorial coverage, so the "≥2 independent sources" bar is met via multiple TMC threads of independent authorship where a Reddit corroboration could not be retrieved.

## Recurring defects

### Exterior

- **Front light bar condensation after washes** — water droplets persist inside the new Juniper front light bar after car washes. Sources: [TMC — "Water droplets/condensation on the front light bar"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/water-droplets-condensation-on-the-front-light-bar-of-a-tesla-model-y-juniper.351617/), [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/). Spans multiple 2025–2026 Juniper deliveries. **Status:** expansion of EXT_019 "Headlight alignment" — existing item mentions "no fogging" but does not call out the new front light bar by name.
- **Rear light bar / tail light bar trim misalignment** — trim around the new full-width light bar sits proud or uneven; Tesla has repeatedly called it "within spec." Sources: [TMC — "Tesla Service said this light bar trim alignment was 'within spec'"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/tesla-service-said-this-light-bar-trim-alignment-was-within-spec.348476/), [TMC — "Juniper rear light bar"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-rear-light-bar.343135/). Spans early and mid-cycle Juniper production. **Status:** new (EXT_020 covers taillight alignment but not the continuous light bar trim).
- **Liftgate / bootgate sits inboard of rear quarter panels** — hatch panel does not sit flush with the body; reported on multiple Juniper deliveries including demo units. Sources: [TMC — "New Model Y (Juniper) misalign bootgate panel?"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/new-model-y-juniper-misalign-bootgate-panel.346448/), [TMC — "Model Y delivery panel gaps"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/model-y-delivery-panel-gaps.341657/). **Status:** expansion of EXT_010 "Trunk/liftgate alignment" — existing spec covers gap symmetry but not flush-to-quarter.
- **Frunk bump-stop misadjustment** — frunk sits proud or uneven because the threaded rubber bump stops were not tuned at the factory. Sources: [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/), [TMC — "Frunk and trunk bump stops loose, how to properly adjust them?"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/frunk-and-trunk-bump-stops-loose-how-to-properly-adjust-them.172710/). **Status:** expansion of EXT_002 "Frunk latch" — latch operation is covered, flush fit is not.
- **Liftgate rattle from loose lower bump stop** — Juniper tailgate rattle traced to a loose lower rubber plug/stop. Sources: [TMC — "Juniper Y tailgate rattles. Video w sound."](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-y-tailgate-rattles-video-w-sound.344286/), [TMC — "Frunk and trunk bump stops loose"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/frunk-and-trunk-bump-stops-loose-how-to-properly-adjust-them.172710/). **Status:** new.
- **Rear hatch water ingress from seam sealant skips** — pre-Juniper but still present on recent Model Ys; missing factory seam sealant behind tailgate lamps is the common culprit. Sources: [TMC — "2023 M-Y Hatch water Ingress"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/2023-m-y-hatch-water-ingress.306855/), [TMC — "Model Y Rear Hatch Leak ****Fix****"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/model-y-rear-hatch-leak-fix.319849/). **Status:** new.

### Tire & wheel

- **Aero / wheel cover rattle and click at low speed** — Juniper wheel cover clips make a clicking sound at low speed, window down. Tesla has acknowledged as a known issue. Sources: [TMC — "Click noise from the wheels"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/click-noise-from-the-wheels.345143/), [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/). **Status:** expansion of WHL_005 "Aero cover fitment" — existing item checks for rattle statically, not at speed.

### Electrical

- **Headlights aimed too high from factory** — long-running Model Y issue where headlights arrive un-aimed. Sources: [TMC — "PSA: New Model Y? Adjust your headlights!"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/psa-new-model-y-adjust-your-headlights.319255/), [TMC — "Tesla headlight adjustment"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/tesla-headlight-adjustment.323686/). **Status:** expansion of EXT_019 — existing item checks pattern evenness but not vertical aim.
- **Rear defrost shipped disabled in software** — new Model Ys have left the factory with rear defrost disabled and must be enabled via service mode. Sources: [TMC — "Rear Defrost Model Y"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/rear-defrost-model-y.288732/), [TMC — "Rear defroster not working"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/rear-defroster-not-working.221119/). **Status:** expansion of INT_031 "Rear window defrost" — existing item assumes defrost is active, does not direct the owner to verify it actually fires.
- **Loose nuts on wheel cowlings inside wheel wells** — four nuts per wheel well reportedly found loose on Juniper, identified as a production issue. Sources: [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/). Single-forum evidence; flagged but see Out of scope.

### Interior

- **Passenger-side ambient light strip misaligned with dash / door** — where the dash accent light meets the door accent light, the two strips do not line up on many Juniper builds. Sources: [TMC — "Ambient lighting alignment"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/ambient-lighting-alignment.346554/), [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/). Spans multiple 2025–2026 Juniper deliveries. **Status:** new (no existing item covers the ambient strip on the new dash).
- **B-pillar / door trim loose from bad clip seating** — pillar and door card trim loosens because clips were not fully seated at assembly. Sources: [TMC — "Rear drivers door interior panel is loose and rattling"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/rear-drivers-door-interior-panel-is-loose-and-rattling.219584/), [TMC — "B Pillar inside cover popping off"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/b-pillar-inside-cover-popping-off.162819/). **Status:** expansion of INT_023 "Door card fit" — existing pulls on handle, does not check the pillar trim.

### Software

- **Phone-key unpairs when parked in home WiFi range** — recurring Juniper bug: phone key drops after ~20 min when parked at home. Sources: [TMC — "Juniper - Phone Key Pairing Keeps Disconnecting"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-phone-key-pairing-keeps-disconnecting.346863/), [TMC — "Juniper phone key problem"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-phone-key-problem.346205/). **Status:** expansion of SFT_007 "Phone key Bluetooth unlock" — existing checks unlock range, not whether the key stays paired after a sit.
- **TCU / WiFi module reboots drop LTE mid-drive** — Juniper premium-data drops every ~15 min while driving; root cause is the WiFi module causing the telematics control unit to reboot. Sources: [TMC — "Model Y Juniper 2026 Connectivity Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/model-y-juniper-2026-connectivity-issues.344461/), [TMC — "Juniper Praise and Issues"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/juniper-praise-and-issues.343366/). **Status:** expansion of SFT_004 "Wi-Fi and LTE connectivity" — existing confirms connectivity at a moment in time, not stability over a drive.

### Documentation

- **Monroney sticker options / price mismatch vs. order** — wrong paint, wrong trim, or FSD missing on the label are all reported. Sources: [TMC — "Was your window sticker correct?"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/was-your-window-sticker-correct.130560/), [TMC — "Incorrect Mulroney sticker"](https://teslamotorsclub.com/tmc/threads/incorrect-mulroney-sticker.129360/). **Status:** reword of DOC_003 "Monroney window sticker" — existing spec says "matches configuration" but does not call out the specific fields most commonly wrong.

## Proposed checklist additions

Written in project voice. Group by existing category id.

- **Exterior:** Inspect front and rear light bars for internal condensation or water droplets after a wash cycle.
- **Exterior:** Confirm rear light bar trim sits flush across its full width with no step at either quarter panel.
- **Exterior:** Verify liftgate panel sits flush to the rear quarter panels, not inboard, with no step at either corner.
- **Exterior:** Check frunk lid sits flush to the fenders on both sides; adjust the threaded rubber bump stops if uneven.
- **Exterior:** Close the liftgate and listen for a rattle from the lower bump stop or plug.
- **Exterior:** Inspect the rear hatch seams behind the tail lights for skipped factory seam sealant.
- **Wheels & tires:** Test the wheel covers at 5–15 mph with the window down; confirm no clicking or rattle from loose clips.
- **Electrical:** Check headlight vertical aim against a wall at 25 feet; both beams should sit at or below headlight center height.
- **Electrical:** Fire the rear defrost from the climate menu and confirm the grid actually heats the glass within 3 minutes.
- **Interior:** Verify the ambient light strip is continuous from dash to door on both sides with no vertical step where the two meet.
- **Interior:** Press firmly on both B-pillar trim covers and all four door cards; confirm no clip pops loose.
- **Software:** Leave the phone key paired, walk away for 20 minutes at home, then confirm the car still unlocks on approach without re-pairing.
- **Software:** Drive for at least 15 minutes and confirm LTE and premium connectivity stay connected without a mid-drive drop.
- **Documents & kit:** On the Monroney, cross-check paint, wheels, interior, motor count, and any FSD or Enhanced Autopilot line against your order agreement line for line.

## Out of scope

- **Loose nuts on wheel cowlings inside wheel wells** — single-thread evidence and not catchable without removing the wheel well liner; fails the no-special-tools bar.
- **Steering wheel micro-vibrations at 70+ mph** — multiple threads, but not catchable on a delivery-day low-speed drive; better suited to a post-delivery shakedown checklist.
- **Front light bar dimness vs. promotional material** — subjective; no objective delivery-day pass/fail.
- **Juniper steering wheel clicking caused by clock spring** — real issue, but catchable by existing ELE_017 / INT_021 behavior checks and often needs a service diagnosis rather than an owner inspection.
- **Headlight "dusty or cloudy" reports** — overlaps with the condensation item above; not a separate check.
